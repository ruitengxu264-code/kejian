# Empirical Methods for Economic Studies

Welcome to the book on *Empirical Methods for Economic Studies*! This book is based on the course taught at School of Business of Renmin University of China (中国人民大学). To a large extent, the current book is a structured collection of my lecture notes. The main novelty of this book is the emphasis on various aspects of scientific methods in empirical studies. Though with a focus on economics-related applications, the underlying principles and ideas are applicable to a wide range of empirical research in social sciences (or even natural sciences). 

In this book, I strive to provide a relatively comprehensive introduction to modern methods for conducting empirical research in economics and related fields such as finance, management, and marketing. To this end, it covers a wider range of topics than most other books which usually focus on a specific strand of empirical methods. In general, it incorporates causal inference, machine learning, algorithms, and structural estimation. The current structure of the book is not finalized, and I will keep updating it as time goes by. Comments and suggestions are welcome. You can contact me at $\texttt{chenzhiyuan@rmbs.ruc.edu.cn}$.
